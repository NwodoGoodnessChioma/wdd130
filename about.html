Our History

Whitewater rafting's history can be traced back to the early 1800s, with early attempts to navigate the Snake River in Wyoming. The first recorded trip on the Snake River in 1811 resulted in it being nicknamed "Mad River" due to its difficulty and danger. In 1843, Lieutenant John Fremont introduced the first rubber raft in the US, followed by improvements by Peter Halkett in 1844. Commercial rafting, becoming a popular activity in the 1960s, saw the emergence of modern whitewater rafts and equipment.
